# Code of Conduct

We are committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free experience for everyone attending Global Security Bootcamp events.

## Our Commitment

Global Security Bootcamp events are dedicated to providing a safe and inclusive community for all attendees, regardless of:
- Background or professional experience
- Gender identity or expression
- Race, ethnicity, or national origin
- Religion or beliefs
- Sexual orientation
- Age or ability
- Employment status or economic background

## Expected Behavior

All attendees, speakers, organizers, and volunteers are expected to:

- **Be respectful** — Treat others with dignity and courtesy.
- **Listen actively** — Value different perspectives and experiences.
- **Ask questions respectfully** — Seek understanding, not confrontation.
- **Avoid assumptions** — Don't assume someone's background or expertise based on appearance or identity.
- **Respect boundaries** — Ask before sharing personal information or touching; respect "no."
- **Contribute positively** — Help create an inclusive, welcoming environment for all.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:

- **Harassment**: Unwelcome comments, jokes, or physical contact based on protected characteristics (race, gender, religion, etc.).
- **Intimidation**: Threats, aggressive language, or behavior designed to silence or exclude.
- **Discrimination**: Treating someone unfairly based on protected characteristics.
- **Sexual harassment**: Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for favors, or other verbal/physical conduct of a sexual nature.
- **Disruptive behavior**: Interrupting sessions, monopolizing conversations, or other conduct that disrupts the event.
- **Unauthorized photography**: Taking or sharing photos/videos without consent.
- **Vendor pressure**: Hard sales pitches or aggressive promotion of products/services.
- **Violation of privacy**: Sharing someone's personal information (name, affiliation, email) without consent.

## Reporting a Concern or Violation

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

### Immediate Action

1. If you feel safe, you can speak to the person directly: "That comment wasn't okay."
2. Notify an event organizer immediately. They can be identified by: [description or badges]
3. If you feel unsafe, step away and ask an organizer for a private conversation.

### Formal Reporting

**During or immediately after the event**:
- Contact [Organizer name] at [email] or [phone]
- Or fill out this [Incident Report Form](link to form)

**Later** (within 1 week):
- Email [Organizer email] with:
  - Description of what happened
  - When and where it occurred
  - Who was involved
  - Any witnesses
  - What impact it had on you

### Confidentiality

- Reports are treated confidentially to the extent possible.
- We will not share your name with the person accused without your consent, except when necessary for safety.
- We will keep you informed of actions taken.

## Enforcement

When a report is received:

1. **We will listen** — We take all reports seriously.
2. **We will investigate** — We may follow up with you and witnesses to understand what happened.
3. **We will act** — Depending on the severity, actions may include:
   - A conversation with the person accused about expected behavior
   - A warning or requirement to leave the event
   - Banning from future Global Security Bootcamp events
   - Reporting to authorities (if laws were violated)

4. **We will follow up** — We will inform you of the outcome (to the extent appropriate).

## Attendee Responsibilities

- Review this Code of Conduct before attending.
- Commit to creating a respectful, inclusive environment.
- Report concerns promptly and in good faith.
- Respect the privacy and dignity of others.

## Organizer Responsibilities

- Brief volunteers on Code of Conduct and reporting procedures.
- Make this Code of Conduct visible (website, event materials, venue).
- Respond promptly and fairly to reports.
- Document incidents for learning and improvement.
- Follow up with affected parties.

## Exceptions

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- Event attendance (in-person and virtual)
- Event-related communications (email, Slack, forums, social media)
- Social events organized as part of the bootcamp

This Code of Conduct does **not** apply to:
- Private conversations unrelated to the event
- Communications on platforms not affiliated with the bootcamp
